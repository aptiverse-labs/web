# Aptiverse paid-ad copy

Read `claims-ledger.md` before changing a line in this file. Every claim
below has been checked against the code. The previous version of this
document sold five features that do not exist, and the caption scripts in
`captions/` still do, so those need the same pass before any video runs.

Read `campaign-strategy.md` for why the audiences are ordered the way
they are.

## Rules that apply to every line here

- **No prices.** Not in a headline, not in a description, not in a
  caption. Price discovery happens on `/pricing`, which renders live from
  the plan catalogue. A hardcoded rand figure in an ad becomes a false
  statement the moment the catalogue changes.
- **No guarantees.** No grade promises, no outcome claims, no invented
  statistics, no "students improved by".
- **No emojis. No em dashes.** Commas, colons, periods.
- **No fear selling to parents. No condescension to students.**
- **No vendor names.** No model names, no payment provider names.
- **Never mention bursaries.** They were removed from the platform.
- **Never mention Cambridge, counsellors, WhatsApp, citations, worked
  solutions, careers matching, or an SBA coach.** See the red list.

**Platform limits.** Meta headline reads cleanly under about 40
characters and truncates past that on some placements, primary text shows
about 125 characters before "see more" on mobile, description about 27.
TikTok text overlay wants under 12 words. Google responsive search ads
take 30-character headlines and 90-character descriptions.

# 1. University students, lead audience

## Core insight

A university student does not fail because they did not work. They fail
because the work went into the wrong place. Nobody tells them which
topics are actually weak, the lecturer's slides are not a syllabus, and
the first real feedback arrives after the first test, which is often too
late for the DP. They are already paying for their own tools and they
decide alone. The purchase does not need anyone's permission.

## Primary promise

Aptiverse works from your actual modules and your actual marks. You build
your courses, you log the assessments you really have, and the practice
you get is generated for those, pitched at your year of study. It refuses
to guess.

## Headline variants

1. Study the topics you are actually losing marks on
2. Your modules. Your marks. Your practice.
3. It only works from your real coursework
4. Know which topics are weak before the test tells you
5. Semester two does not have to go like semester one

## Primary body copy, ad length

> Add your modules, log the assessments you actually have, and Aptiverse
> generates practice for those, at your year of study. It will not
> generate anything until there is real work to generate it from. Your
> weak topics come from your own results, not a guess. Start free.

Short version, under the mobile fold:

> Add your modules and real assessments. Get practice built for them,
> at your level. Your weak topics come from your own results.

## Short-form social variant

**TikTok or Reels, on-screen text, one line per beat.**

> Everyone says study smarter.
> Nobody tells you which topics are weak.
> Add your modules. Log the test that's coming.
> It builds the practice from that.
> Not generic. Yours.
> Free to start.

**Static, one line:**

> Most study apps guess. This one waits until you give it your real work.

## Call to action

"Start free". Secondary: "See how it works".

## Objection it must overcome

"I already have AI, and it is free." The answer is not that Aptiverse has
better AI. It is that a general chatbot has no idea what is on your test,
what you already know, or where your marks are going, and it will happily
invent a syllabus. Aptiverse will not generate practice at all until you
have given it a real module and a real assessment. That constraint is the
product.

Second objection: "another subscription". Answered by the free tier and
by never leading with price.

## Landing page

`/for-students`. Test against `/register` per A/B test 3.

## Angle variants to run against each other

**Angle A, wasted effort.** "You did the work. It went into the wrong
topics." Not seasonal, works all year, strongest hypothesis.

**Angle B, first-year reality.** "First year is not harder. It is just
nobody checks on you." Aimed at the registration and orientation window
and at the January supplementary period. Honest about the drop-off
without being grim about it.

**Angle C, exam window.** "Three weeks to the exam. Which topics are
actually weak?" Sharp, seasonal, run in the four to five weeks before
each examination season and then stop.

**Angle D, second chance.** "Semester one is done. Semester two starts
now." Run in July, aimed at the student who has just seen a mark they did
not want.

## Institution-named variants, A/B test 5

Named institutions sharpen relevance at the cost of audience size.
Institution names in the copy must be geographic and factual, never
implying a partnership or endorsement.

> Studying at Wits? Add your modules, log your tests, and practise the
> topics your own results say are weak.

Rotate: UCT, Wits, Stellenbosch, UP, UJ, UKZN, NWU, Rhodes, UWC, TUT,
CPUT, DUT, UNISA. UNISA deserves its own treatment: distance students are
the most isolated segment in the country and the "nobody checks on you"
angle lands hardest there, but their assessment calendar differs, so run
it as a separate ad set.

## What this audience must never be sold

CAPS, NSC, matric, SBA, terms or grades. Their year is semesters,
modules, coursework and DP. The AI tutor already switches vocabulary by
student type, so the ads must match what the product does when they
arrive.

# 2. Parents, second audience

High-school students are reached here, through the parent who pays. They
do not get their own paid headline set.

## Core insight

A parent of a high-school child gets information twice a term, in a
report, long after anything could have been done about it. What they want
is not surveillance and not a daily alert. They want to know whether the
year is going the way it needs to, early enough to act, without turning
every conversation at home into an interrogation.

The South African version of this has a hard edge: school-based
assessment accumulates through the year, so a meaningful share of the
final mark is fixed before the final examinations start. A parent who
only finds out at the end has found out too late by design.

## Primary promise

A read-only view of how your child is actually doing, shared with their
consent, built from work they have really done. Not a tracker. Not a
notification stream. A shared picture.

## Headline variants

1. Know how the year is going, before the report
2. Your child shares it. You can see it.
3. Built from real marks, not a guess
4. Visibility, not surveillance
5. The marks are already accumulating

## Primary body copy, ad length

> Your child logs their assessments and practises the topics they are
> weakest at. With their consent, you get a read-only overview: subjects,
> topic strengths, where the marks are heading. No tracking, no reading
> their messages, no alerts at midnight. Start free.

Short version:

> With your child's consent, see how the year is really going: subjects,
> weak topics, where the marks are heading. Read-only.

## Short-form social variant

**Facebook feed, single image:**

> The report card arrives twice a term. The marks accumulate every week.

**Longer social, for a parenting group placement:**

> Most of us find out how the year went when the report arrives. By then
> a large part of the final mark is already fixed, because school-based
> assessment accumulates all year. Aptiverse gives your child a place to
> log their assessments and practise what they are weakest at, and gives
> you a read-only overview, if they accept the invitation. They keep their
> diary private. You get the picture, not a feed.

## Call to action

"See how it works". Secondary: "Start free".

## Objection it must overcome

Two, and they pull in opposite directions.

The child's objection, which the parent anticipates: "this is spying".
Answered by the consent model, which is real. The parent sends an
invitation, the child accepts or declines, the overview is read-only, and
the wellbeing diary is not in it. Say that plainly, because it is the
strongest thing this offer has.

The parent's own objection: "will they actually use it?" Answered by not
overselling. Do not promise engagement. Point at the free tier and let
them test it.

## Landing page

`/for-families`.

## Tone constraint, absolute

No fear. No "is your child falling behind", no countdown to matric, no
implication of parental failure. A parent buying out of anxiety churns
the moment the anxiety passes, and it is a bad way to treat people. The
register is calm and practical.

## Where high school appears

Inside this section only. The child-facing product is real and strong for
high school: CAPS subjects, SBA tasks with uploads, practice built from
those, topic mastery, term projections, workspace drafts, study groups,
a private diary. Copy may describe all of that as what the child gets.
It is simply not bought by the child, so it is not addressed to them in
paid media during this phase.

# 3. Tutors, supply side

Runs from day one on a small fixed budget. Judged on completed profiles
and first proposals, not on subscription revenue.

## Core insight

South African tutoring runs on word of mouth, which caps a tutor at the
size of their own network. The platforms that offer to widen it take a
commission on every hour worked. Aptiverse takes nothing, on any plan,
including the free one, because it never sits between the tutor and the
money.

## Primary promise

List a profile at no cost, be found by students and families who are
already looking, answer real requests on the jobs board, and keep
everything you earn.

## Headline variants

1. Get found. Keep every rand.
2. We never take a cut. Not on any plan.
3. Students post what they need. You answer.
4. Your profile, your rates, your money
5. Tutoring work, without the commission

## Primary body copy, ad length

> List a free profile with your subjects, qualifications and rates, and
> be found by students and parents already searching. Students post what
> they need on the jobs board and you send a proposal. You arrange the
> lessons and you are paid directly. Aptiverse never takes a cut.

Short version:

> Free profile. Real requests from students. You arrange the lessons and
> keep everything you earn.

## Short-form social variant

> Every tutoring platform takes a cut of your hour.
> This one takes nothing.
> Free profile. Students post what they need. You answer.
> You get paid directly, by them.

## Call to action

"List your profile free".

## Objection it must overcome

"What is the catch?" A marketplace that takes no commission sounds like a
bait. Answer it in the ad, not after the click: Aptiverse never handles
the payment between a tutor and a family, so there is nothing to take a
cut of. The paid tutor plans buy placement and prep tools, not the right
to keep your income.

Second objection: "will I actually get work?" Never promise volume.
Discoverability is real, enquiries are not guaranteed, and claiming
otherwise burns the exact people the marketplace depends on.

## Landing page

`/for-tutors`.

## What may not be claimed to tutors

AI lesson plans, AI worksheets, automatic parent reports, per-client
mastery tracking, an AI SBA marker, SARS income exports, and WhatsApp
anything. All of those are unbuilt entitlement flags. The real paid-tier
benefits are placement in search, the curriculum-aware AI for a tutor's
own preparation, higher AI limits, and profile insight. Sell those.

## University student overlap

Postgraduate students tutoring undergraduates are the cheapest tutor
acquisition available and they are already in the lead audience. A
tutor-side variant aimed at postgraduates should run inside the campus
motion rather than as a separate media buy.

# 4. Schools, deferred

**Do not run paid media to schools in this phase.** The teacher and
school-admin surfaces are not built: classes, assignments, verifications,
school analytics and readiness reports all return empty today. Selling
into a school now would be selling a shell, and it would consume budget
that needs to be proving unit economics on a self-serve audience.

Inbound will still arrive. `/contact` handles school enquiries and the
enquiry pipeline exists. Copy for that page and for a reply to an inbound
enquiry only:

> Aptiverse is built for individual students, families and tutors today.
> The whole-school deployment, with teacher tools and school-level
> reporting, is in development and sold directly rather than self-serve.
> Tell us about your school and we will come back to you when there is
> something real to show you.

Honest, keeps the relationship, promises nothing that does not exist.

Conditions that open this phase are in `campaign-strategy.md`.

# Creative pairing

The visual ad units live in `web/src/app/(marketing)/ads/`, owned by
another agent. Pairing guidance, subject to the claims ledger:

- University angles A and C: the mastery view, weak topics surfacing from
  real results. Not a rising line implying an outcome.
- University angle B and D: the course setup flow, a student adding their
  own modules. This is the tertiary difference made visible.
- Parent: the read-only overview with the consent step visible in frame.
  The invitation and the accept are the product's best moment. Show them.
- Tutors: the jobs board with real listings and the proposal flow.

Scenes that must not be used until the underlying feature exists: any
citation chip, any worked-solution walk-through, any counsellor, any
confidence band, any WhatsApp surface, any teacher or school dashboard.

# Caption scripts

`captions/aptiverse-30s.srt`, `-60s.srt` and `-90s.srt` all currently sell
counsellors, citations, examiner-style past paper marking, an SBA coach,
a matric mark forecast, Cambridge, and tutor AI lesson plans with WhatsApp
parent reports. Every one of those is on the red list. The scripts need a
full rewrite against the claims ledger, and they need reframing for the
university lead audience, before any video runs. They are not usable as
they stand.

# A/B priorities

Run the university angles first, one variable at a time, per the test
table in `campaign-strategy.md`. Angle A against angle C is the first
test, because it separates the evergreen message from the seasonal one
and that determines how the rest of the year is planned. Tutors run in
parallel on their own small budget and their own funnel. Parents wait for
phase 2.

Nothing scales on clicks. Nothing scales at all until conversion tracking
exists, which it does not yet.
